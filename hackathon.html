Hackathon

Our Goal

The Black Girl STEM Brilliance Hackathon 2021 is based on the theme of Women Empowerment. It is important for the rising generation of Women in STEM to feel nurtured and guided throughout this Hackathon and to understand the significance that being a woman in STEM and especially a Woman of Color in STEM needs to be more prominent and have various levels of representation. This Theme will allow these students to explore their imagination and create programs that relate to the Empowerment of Women and helping a Woman in STEM be successful in their field.

A javaScript based Hackathon that guides students through coding, but also gives students the freedom to use their creativity based on difficulty level with the help of skilled Computer Science mentors per group . Out of the Maximum of 350 students participating There are 4-5 students per Groups will be split by grade level (6-12) and 3 varying levels of prompt difficulty: Beginner Level, Intermediate Level, Expert Level.

Rules

  • Teams should be made up exclusively of students who are not organizers, volunteers, judges, sponsors, or in any other privileged position at the event.
  • All team members should be present at the event. Leaving the venue (virtual-discord platform) for some time to hack elsewhere is fine.
  • Teams can of course gain advice and support from organizers, volunteers, sponsors, and others.
  • All work on a project should be done at the hackathon.
  • Teams can work on an idea that they have worked on before (as long as they do not reuse code)
  • Teams can use an idea they had before the event.
  • Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers’ discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to breaking the Competition Rules, behaving in a way that violates the code of conduct or other unsporting behaviour.
  • Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
  • Please treat all other hackers with the utmost respect. We ask that you act kindly, behave professionally, and do not insult or put down other attendees. Remember that harassment and racist, sexist, or exclusionary jokes are not appropriate for this event. If at any point you see a fellow hacker being harassed, please talk to the nearest hackathon organizer. (DEROGATORY LANGUAGE OR ACTIONS WILL NOT BE TOLERATED)
  • If you are to join a different group’s server or select the wrong grade level you will be penalized and not receive a prize.
  • When a speaker is speaking in the live stream channel do not interrupt or text anything in the text channel, that is only intended for the help channel and between you and your volunteer/ sponsor who is meant to aid your project.
